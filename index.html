Ives (Hongqing) Xue

Statistics & Economics @ Wake Forest University

Hello! I am Ives (Hongqing) Xue, a senior at Wake Forest University pursuing a B.S. in Statistics and Economics with a minor in Mathematics.

My primary research interest lies in statistical learning, with a focus on building robust and generalizable models under complex data environments. I am broadly interested in causal inference, biostatistics, and environmental economics, particularly in applications to public health and policy.

My recent work explores distribution shift, heterogeneous treatment effects, and environmental exposure modeling, combining theoretical rigor with applied data analysis.

Research Experience

  • Generalizable Classification under Label Shift
    Developed and evaluated methods (BBSE, MLLS) for classification under distribution shift. Built pipelines for selecting shift-stable features and robust predictive models.
  • Downwind Effects of Highways and Power Plants on EMS (NYC)
    Constructed wind-based exposure indices and designed models to study environmental impacts on emergency medical service demand and environmental justice outcomes.
  • Dirty Wind and EMS Demand (Causal Forest Analysis)
    Built large-scale panel datasets and applied causal forests to estimate heterogeneous treatment effects across neighborhoods and time.
  • Mathematical Model for Syphilis with Reinfection
    Developed an ODE-based epidemiological model incorporating reinfection, treatment, and immunity dynamics.
  • Collider Bias Simulation Study
    Conducted simulation studies to analyze collider bias and evaluate mitigation strategies in causal inference.
